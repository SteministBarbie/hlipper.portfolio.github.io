About Me

As a teenager in the 1990s, I was swept up by a passion for musical and creative self-expression. All of my friends heard the same whisperings that I did. Who knows from where they sprung? Still, the message was clear. Move to Chicago. Chicago is about to become the next Seattle... As soon as I could, I scraped together enough money to rent a run-down Victorian storefront with four guys and a three-legged cat. I rehearsed six days a week, convinced my hard work would pay off eventually; someday I would be able to tap into a bit of the magic I felt listening to music written by others.

Years went by. I worked desk jobs to pay the bills. I began to recognize a factor I hadn’t yet considered in how different bands, offering up relatively similar experiences and content, were perceived by audiences. I started to understand that the most successful acts had a designer among their ranks, or waiting in the wings, who could skillfully modulate the perception of the band’s standard indie rock fare with images, typography, color and words.

I tried to understand what was affecting us. I visited museums, read books, but found nothing much that could really clue me in on the mechanics of it. My music was heard by few.

Eventually, after years of false starts and other minor disasters, my musical peer group began to fall away. One by one, old friends I relied on for feedback and support jumped ship to take on more traditional roles. They scattered across the country, started families, and I was left to do some serious identity work. I could not abandon my desire to focus on meaningful, creative projects and dig deeper into the realm of ideas. Who am I really? What is my way forward? Circumstances forced me to ask these things of myself.

I come to the design field with twenty years of work experience, bundled with an exciting new parcel of developing skills. I have more than a little grit stuck in my hair and under my nails at this point, and I can bring all of this and more to your projects.
