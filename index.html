About Me

Ancient History

As a teenager in the 1990s, I was swept up by a passion for musical and creative self-expression. Rumor had it Chicago was about to become the next Seattle. As soon as I could, I scraped together enough money to rent a run-down Victorian storefront with four guys and a three-legged cat. I rehearsed six days a week, convinced my hard work would pay off eventually; someday I would be able to tap into a bit of the magic I felt listening to music written by others.

Years went by. I worked desk jobs to pay the bills. I began to recognize a factor I hadn’t yet considered in how local bands, offering up relatively similar experiences and content, were perceived by audiences. I started to understand that the most successful acts had a designer among their ranks, or waiting in the wings, who could skillfully modulate the perception of the band’s standard indie rock fare with images, typography, color and words. I tried to understand what was affecting us.

Life Goes On

Eventually, after years of false starts and other minor disasters, my musical peer group began to fall away. One by one, old friends I relied on for feedback and support jumped ship to take on more traditional roles. They scattered across the country, started families, and I was left to do some serious identity work. I could not abandon my desire to focus on meaningful reative work and ideas.

A Way Forward...

I come to the design field with twenty years of work experience, bundled with an exciting new parcel of developing skills. I have an undying fascination with human creative self-expression - and a need to solve it’s many mysteries. I can bring all of this and more to your projects.
